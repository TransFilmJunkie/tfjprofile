Hardware Builds

I sratchbuilt this retro 486-powered PC from mostly period-correct parts, although I did add a few modern conviniences, such as using a CF card for a hard drive, due to the finicky nature of vintage drives. The case is a clone of the IBM XT that seems to have been originally intended for use as a student project computer judging by the case's surviving documentation.

      ╫

Specs:

  • Case: Simplex XT
  • Motherboard: Shuttle HOT-419DZ VLB
  • Processor: Intel 486DX2 66Mhz
  • RAM: 4MB Kingston SIMM X4 (16MB)
  • Graphics: BOCA SuperX VGA VLB 1MB
  • Sound: Soundblaster AWE-32
  • I/O Card: Data Technology Corp. DTC2278D Rev. A IDE & Super I/O Drive Controller Card
  • Storage: 2GB CF Card
  • Drives: 3.5" Floppy, 5.25" Floppy, CD-ROM

This machine is my baby, and my current modern PC. At the time that I built it, the market was experiencing a major shortage of graphics cards (due to the chip shortages and the time being the peak of Bitcoin mining with graphics cards), so I ended up cannibalizing some parts from an HP pre-built PC for this project. In addition, I was able to re-use some components from my previous PC as well. Due to this nature, I have nicknamed it the "Wedding PC", since it uses something old (previous PC parts), something new, something borrowed (components from HP prebuilt), and something blue (LED lights).

      ╫

Specs:

  • Case: Phanteks Enthoo Pro Full Tower
  • Motherboard: MSI MPG X570 Gaming Plus
  • Processor: AMD Ryzen 5 3500
  • CPU Cooler: Cooler Master Hyper 212 RGB Black Edition
  • RAM: 16GB DDR4-2666MHz (1x Corsair 8GB and 1x HP 8GB)
  • Graphics: Nvidia GTX 1650 Super
  • Storage: Total of 6 drives with 2x WD Blue 1TB SSD, 1x WD Blue 500GB SSD, 1x 1TB HDD, 1x 600GB HDD, 1x 237GB NVME SSD
  • Drives: 3.5" Floppy via internal USB card and adaptor, ASUS DVD-ROM Drive
  • PSU: be quiet! Straight Power 11 Platinum 550W PSU
